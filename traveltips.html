Attraction

Cebu is a province in the Philippines that offers a wide range of attractions for tourists. From historical landmarks to natural wonders, there's something for everyone.

  • Magellan's Cross - a historical landmark marking the arrival of Spanish conquistador Ferdinand Magellan in 1521.
  • Basilica del Santo Niño - the oldest Roman Catholic church in the Philippines, dedicated to the Child Jesus.
  • Fort San Pedro - a military defense structure built by Spanish conquistador, Miguel López de Legazpi.
  • Cebu Taoist Temple - a beautiful temple that offers a panoramic view of the city.
  • Osmeña Peak - the highest peak in Cebu, offering a stunning view of the island's landscape.

Food

Cebu is known for its delicious food, with a mix of Filipino and Spanish influences. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Lechon - a roasted pig dish that's a staple in Filipino celebrations.
  • Sutukil - a combination of three cooking methods: sugba (grilled), tuwa (soup), and kilaw (ceviche).
  • Cebuano BBQ - a grilled meat dish that's marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and pepper.
  • Ngohiong - a spring rollfilled with minced pork, vegetables, and shrimp.
  • Bibingka - a rice cake made with coconut milk, sugar, and eggs, usually served during Christmas season.

Transportation

Getting around Cebu is relatively easy, with various modes of transportation available.

  • Jeepneys - the most common mode of transportation in Cebu, with fixed routes and affordable fares.
  • Buses - available for long-distance travel, with air-conditioned and non-air-conditioned options.
  • Taxis - widely available, with metered fares and a higher cost compared to jeepneys and buses.
  • Grab - the Southeast Asian version of Uber, available for booking through the Grab app.
  • Habal-habal - a motorcycle taxi that can accommodate up to two passengers, usually used for short distances and steep roads.

Accommodation

Cebu has a wide range of accommodations to choose from, depending on your budget and preference.

  • Hotels - from budget to luxury, there are plenty of hotels to choose from in Cebu.
  • Resorts - for a more relaxed and laid-back vacation, there are several resorts in Cebu that offer a wide range of amenities and activities.
  • Hostels - for budget travelers, there are several hostels in Cebu that offer affordable accommodations.
  • B&Bs - for a more personalized and intimate experience, there are several B&Bs in Cebu that offer comfortable accommodations and home-cooked meals.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you make the most out of your trip to Cebu:

  • Pack light - Cebu has a tropical climate, so it's best to pack light and comfortable clothing.
  • Bring enough cash - while there are plenty of ATMs in Cebu, it's still best to bring enough cash for your trip.
  • Try the local food - Cebuano cuisine is a must-try when visiting Cebu. Don't be afraid to try new dishes and flavors.
  • Respect local customs and traditions - Cebu is a predominantly Catholic province, so it's important to respect local customs and traditions.
  • Stay safe - always prioritize your safety when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid dangerous areas.
