Malapascua Island

Situated in the northern part of Cebu Province, Philippines, Malapascua Island is a picturesque tropical paradise renowned for its stunning beaches, vibrant coral reefs, and the opportunity to encounter thresher sharks, making it a haven for divers and beachgoers alike.

Diving Destination

Malapascua Island is internationally recognized as one of the best diving destinations in the Philippines, particularly for its chance to encounter thresher sharks. Monad Shoal, a submerged seamount located near the island, is one of the few places in the world where divers can reliably see these majestic creatures in their natural habitat. Other dive sites around the island offer opportunities to explore colorful coral gardens, underwater caves, and diverse marine life, including manta rays, hammerhead sharks, and numerous species of reef fish.

Pristine Beaches

Malapascua Island is blessed with pristine white-sand beaches and crystal-clear waters that are perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and beachcombing. The main beach, Bounty Beach, is lined with resorts, restaurants, and beach bars, offering a lively atmosphere during the day and stunning sunset views in the evening. For those seeking a more secluded experience, quieter beaches like Logon Beach and Langob Beach provide a tranquil escape from the crowds.

Island Hopping

Beyond its spectacular diving and beautiful beaches, Malapascua Island is surrounded by a cluster of smaller islands and islets that are perfect for island-hopping adventures. Visitors can charter boats to explore nearby destinations such as Kalanggaman Island, known for its pristine sandbar and turquoise lagoon, or Gato Island, a marine sanctuary with excellent snorkeling and diving opportunities.

Local Culture

Despite its popularity as a tourist destination, Malapascua Island maintains a laid-back, relaxed vibe, with friendly locals and a strong sense of community. Visitors can immerse themselves in the island's local culture by sampling traditional Filipino cuisine, participating in cultural activities and festivals, or simply chatting with residents to learn about their way of life.

Sustainable Tourism

As a popular diving and eco-tourism destination, Malapascua Island is committed to promoting sustainable practices to preserve its natural beauty and marine ecosystems. Local dive operators and resorts adhere to responsible diving guidelines to minimize environmental impact, while community-based initiatives focus on marine conservation, waste management, and eco-friendly tourism practices.
